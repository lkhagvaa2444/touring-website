Itinerary

Day 1
Drive from Ulaanbaatar to Khar Khorin the ancient capital city of the Mongolian Empire. Here you will see the largest Buddhist Monastery in Mongolia, Eredene Zuu.  Wake up early to see the monks chanting in the morning and to visit the museum which tells of the creation of this once great capital city.
Day 2
Tuvkhun Khiid is a more remote monastery that requires a 4 km hike. Tuvkhun Monastery is one of Mongolia’s oldest Buddhist monasteries, situated at the top of mountains. Tuvkhun Monastery was first established in 1648 by the 14 year old Zanabazar, the first Jebtsundamba Khutuktu, the spiritual head of Tibetan Buddhism.

The monastery was destroyed in 1688, and restored in 1773. Restoration of the monastery was completed in 1997. Ceremonies were staged to re-consecrate the monastery and a new statue of Gombo Makhagal (Mahakala). Several monks now reside and practice at the monastery full-time.
Day 3 – 7
8 Lake Trek begins. Trek by horse or by foot through this magnificent landscape. You will see the wilds of Mongolia, mountain passes, lush valleys, thick forests and a plethora of birds and other wildlife.

The 8 lakes area is comprised of Shireet, the largest and the most beautiful, Khaliut, Bugat, Khaya, Khuis, Onon, Doroo and Bayan Uul. Two of the smallest lakes are completely dry, and the second largest lake, Khuis Nuur, is 90% dry.
Day 8
Finish the trek and stay with a nomadic family that night. Rest and recover from your epic adventure and begin the journey back to Ulaanbaatar. You will stay at Khogon Khan valley and have the option to hike deep into the valley to a monastery hidden from the soviets and protected from destruction.
Day 9
Drive back to UB through the fertile steppe of central Mongolia.
