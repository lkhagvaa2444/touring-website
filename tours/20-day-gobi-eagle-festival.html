20 Day – Gobi / Eagle Festival

Travel across Mongolia through the heart of the country and arrive in Bayan Olgii, where you will witness a truly ancient profession: Eagle Hunting.

Trip Info

Visit the spectacular Gobi Desert and experience the wonder of one of the most northern deserts in the world. You will see geological and natural wonders like nothing you’ve ever seen before.

Travel across Mongolia through the heart of the country and arrive in Bayan Olgii, where you will witness a truly ancient profession; Eagle Hunting is one of the most noble and awe inspiring unions of beast and man. Get an inside look at this truly ancient and wonderful art.

Itinerary

Day 1 – UB – Baga Gazriin
Chuluu
Baga Gazriin Chuluu is a range of granite hills surrounded by the desert. It is believed Chinggis Khaan grazed his horses here. These hills are a good place for scrambling and hiking. On the way to Baga Gazriin Chuluu, we will visit Zorgol Khairkhan Mountain. The mountain is venerated and revered by the locals, who believe it is the home of a great, benevolent spirit.
Day 2 – Tsagaan Suvarga
This place is about 30 meters tall and 100 meters long and shaped very strangely, a result of wind and water erosion over thousands of years. From a distance, it can be mistaken for the ruins of an ancient city.
Day 3 – Yoliin Am /
Bayanzag
The narrow Valley between high rock walls has breathtaking dramatic scenery, and no doubt is one of the most beautiful places in the country. Bayanzag is a world renowned for the dinosaur bones and eggs discovered there. Archeologists claim that the area has also a lot of unearthed objects from the Stone Age.
Day 4 – Khongoryn Els
The 400-meter high sand dunes in Sevrei soum, these dunes are 20 km wide and 100 km long. The Khongoryn River flows along the sand dunes and gives birth to an oasis.
Day 5 – Naranbulag
Arashaan
These springs or Arshaans hold powerful spiritual meaning for many Mongolians. They are known to cure many ailments depending on the location of the spring. 280km.
Day 6 – Khermen Tsav
Kherem means ‘wall,’ and tsav means ‘cracked’ or ‘broken.’ This cracked wall of mud rock stretches from the northwest to the southwest. The formation of Khermen Tsav resembles unfinished artistic architecture and well-designed red brick buildings of ancient cities. Many dinosaur fossils were found here among the saxaul forest and desert plants. 80km.
Day 7 – Tsagaan Agui
Bayan Gobi soum. Here we will visit Tsagaan cave, located in Tsakhir Mountain. This cave housed Stone Age man some 700,000 years ago, and has been the site of numerous archeological digs. 280km
Day 8 – Boon Tsagaan
Nuur
This large saltwater lake, at the end of Baidrag Gol river, is popular with birdlife, especially the relic gull, whooper swan and geese. The region also boasts extraordinary volcanic formations, canyons of cascading streams and ancient cave paintings.
Day 9 –Biger Nuur
This area is home to a vineyard, one of the few in Mongolia. Rock carvings are also present here. 20km.
Day 10 – Khar Us Nuur
The park located near the Great Lakes’ Depression Area (Ikh Nuuruudiin Khotgor) covering a large area of desert steppe and arid semi-desert environment. It is a home and last refuge for rare wildlife species such as migratory pelicans, falcons, bustards, wild ducks, geese, wood grouse, partridges and seagulls, including the rare relict gull and herring gull. Although it is the second largest fresh water lake in the country, the lake’s average depth is only 4 meter. Khovd River flows into this lake, creating a giant marsh delta. The park was included in the list of the Ramsar Convention in 1999 due to its suitable environment for water and marsh bird species. This area is comprised of 850,272 hectares of land in Khovd Province and it was designated as a national park in 1997.
Day 11 – Free day at the
lake
Camp on the way to Khuvsgul province (there is also the option to stay with a local family).
Day 12 – Khovd
Khovd is distinguished by its multi-cultural population. It is home to more than 17 nationalities and ethnicities
Day 13 – Bayan Ulgii
Most inhabitants of Bayan-Ölgii are Kazakhs (88.7%).A significant portion of the population speaks Kazakh as their mother tongue and the Mongolian language only as a second language, if at all. The culture of the Kazakh majority is strongly influenced by Islamic traditions.
Day 14 – Altai Tavan Bogd
Camp under the stars in the mountains!
Day 15 – Altai Tavan Bogd
The national park contains the country's highest peaks,  and a wonderfully beautiful glacier.
Day 16 – Khuiten Peak
Khuiten Peak is the highest point in Mongolia. The Peak towers at the elevation of 4,374 m (14,350 feet) under the permanent snow-cap. It is one of five peaks in the National Park (five saints), together with the Friendship Peak (Nairamdal), the Bürged Uul (Eagle Mountain), the Naran Uul, and the Ölgii Uul.
Day 17 – Camp enroute to
Ulgii
Enjoy the beauty and harshness of Western Mongolia’s wild countryside.
Day 18-19 – Eagle Festival
The festival schedule will be posted before the festival. Expect to see some spectacular feats of control and cooperation between bird and man. This two day event is host to so many visual spectacles it is hard to comprehend them all! Races, flying competitions, tug of war, archery and much more will be available for viewing. There are food tents here as well as souvenirs and even opportunities to take a photo holding and eagle!
Day 20 – Travel back to UB
With fond memories of an amazing Mongolian adventure you will head back to Ulaanbaatar by flight out of Olgii.
